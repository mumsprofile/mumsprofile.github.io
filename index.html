Research Papers

My research explores the intersection of biology and health, with a particular focus on sustainable food practices, sports science, and medical challenges in Ghana. Through these papers, I aim to address real-world issues while contributing to solutions that benefit both individuals and communities.

Research Paper 1: Protecting Human Bones and Reducing Injury Risk in Ghanaian Athletes

Overview: This study focuses on developing injury prevention strategies for athletes.

My Role: Lead researcher and data analyst working closely with sports coaches to revise training protocols.

Methodology: Implemented strength training, dynamic warm-ups, and biomechanical assessments to monitor injury rates.

Outcome: Results contributed to improved athletic performance and reduced minor injuries by 15% in the Kumasi Academy soccer team.

Research Paper 2: The Nutritional Value of Vegetables and Their Preservation: Impacts on Human Health and Sustainability

Overview: This research examines the importance of preserving vegetables to improve nutrition, reduce waste, and create economic opportunities for local communities. It highlights how vegetable preservation benefits families, including my own, in Ghana.

My Role: Lead researcher investigating spoilage mechanisms and preservation techniques.

Methodology: Analyzed nutritional value pre- and post-preservation using laboratory tests, interviewed local farmers, and studied spoilage patterns.

Outcome: Found effective methods to extend shelf life, which has contributed to job creation in my community.

Link to Research Paper

Research Paper 3: Bloodstream Infections in Cancer Patients in Ghana

Overview: This paper sheds light on the types of infections affecting cancer patients in Ghana and offers targeted solutions for better treatment protocols.

My Role: Conducted microbiological research to identify common infections in cancer patients.

Methodology: Used lab tests to identify infections and collaborated with doctors to recommend targeted treatments.

Outcome: Improved understanding of how to treat infections in immunocompromised patients, contributing to better health outcomes.
